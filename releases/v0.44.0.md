- Tier A: a clean grep proves the PATTERN, not the FILE (R59 — fourth of the family): watcher.md § Rules — scope every claim to what was measured; a status board reports its COVERAGE alongside its result, and a result whose coverage is unstated is not a result. The watcher's PHI board called two entries clean that were genuinely dirty (a different leak SHAPE in the same file it had checked with a narrower pattern; and one entry it had never grepped at all), and a builder's fresh grep caught both. Same act as grep-the-symptom and path-presence-as-content: reporting confidently beyond what was measured — by the chair that demanded coverage statements from everyone else.

- Tier A: VERIFY BY ENUMERATION, NEVER BY PATTERN-MATCH + a security comment NAMES ITS PIN (R60, fifth of the family): root CLAUDE.md § PHI — read the log call and judge EVERY field in it. A grep for known-bad field names is a REGRESSION check and is structurally blind to DISCOVERY: a sibling field in the very call it just cleared, under a name nobody has banned yet, is invisible to it. (The watcher's board called a call clean while a sibling field in that same call still leaked patient text at INFO; the judge caught it.) AND, third occurrence of the class: a comment asserting a security property must NAME THE PIN that proves it, or it does not ship — a comment claiming a protection it lacks is a lie waiting to be believed, and naming its pin converts the claim from believable into checkable.

- Tier B: a BRIEF quotes its manifest VERBATIM, never looser (honest-absence R1): orchestrator.md § O3 Build truth — a loose restatement invites a builder to satisfy the BRIEF and miss the spec. The manifest is the truth; the BRIEF is a pointer to it. Where they differ the manifest wins, and a builder that chooses the manifest over the BRIEF is RIGHT — the BRIEF is the defect. (Caught because a builder did exactly that: it took the manifest's "no new retry key" over the BRIEF's looser phrasing, unprompted.)

- Tier A: AUTOMATION MAKES A CHECK CONSISTENT, NOT COMPLETE (R61 — the enumerator's own blind spot): the new PHI-in-logs lint shipped blind to a CHAINED logger call (`logger.bind(...).warning(...)` — a receiver that is itself a call), and a real leak sat there, one nesting level below where it looked, beside an already-exempted field on the same call. An enumerator is only as good as its definition of "everything", and it will miss the same shape EVERY TIME — reliably, silently, at scale, wearing the authority of a machine. root CLAUDE.md § PHI: a checker DECLARES the shapes it covers, PINS each one, and NAMES the shapes it does not — a clean result from an unstated coverage is the same lie in a machine's voice. The law demanded of the auditor, failed on the watcher's own board, now binds the lint rule: one rule at three altitudes.

- Tier A: the diff-card reader must see UNTRACKED files (R62 — the spine): `git diff` is blind to untracked files, so a diff-only reader reported five existing test files as "no test file at all" — it converted *outside my coverage* into *does not exist*, which is this codebase's own honest-absence disease wearing a tool's face. orchestrator.md § O4.1 — the reader runs `git diff` AND `git status --porcelain`, and NAMES what its instrument could not see. THE UNIFYING SENTENCE OF THE NIGHT: the frontend renders a failed query as "no data"; a grep renders a pattern-miss as "clean"; the PHI lint renders an unenumerated call-shape as "no leak"; `git diff` renders an untracked file as "no test file at all" — every one of them reporting *outside my coverage* as *does not exist*. The disease cured in the product lived in every instrument used to cure it. The only thing that ever caught it, at every altitude, was an independent instrument whose coverage was stated: the judge must never be the thing being judged.

- Tier A: THE WAVE'S OWN SCOPE IS A COVERAGE CLAIM (honest-absence R2 — the disease inside the cure, at the scope altitude): orchestrator.md § O2 — for a wave that cures a CLASS (every error-renders-as-absence, every PHI-in-a-log, every unguarded X), the reconcile enumerates EVERY reachable instance per surface and maps each to a task or an EXPLICIT defer. An instance nobody claims is not "out of scope" — it is an UNSTATED HOLE, and it will survive the very wave written to kill it. Caught live: the honest-absence wave was shipping with a reachable absence-render (a composite empty-state reached via a pipeline-step failure) that no task claimed.

- Tier A: AN EMPTY ENUMERATION IS NEVER A VERDICT (R63 — the framework-instrument audit; we went looking on purpose): FOUR HIGH instruments whose "clean" is silent about partial scope — the security audit, the O2 reconcile, the officer, and qa-fe — all failing identically. PLUS a real defect in the WAVE-WALKER, the instrument every gate trusts most: `threads.length === 0` over a NON-EMPTY diff still shipped SMOOTH SAILING (no guard existed). The devastating detail: the walker ALREADY guards the empty-DIFF case, with the law perfectly stated in its own comment — "a walk over nothing must never return a verdict" — and never applied it one altitude up. Fixed: zero-thread guard added in the same words (syntax-verified). Root CLAUDE.md: every instrument reporting "nothing found" emits a COVERAGE LINE naming what it inspected and what it SKIPPED, and none may render an empty enumeration as "clean" — a scout that found no threads has proven only that IT could not see.

- Park (/jc, quiet board): km-guard.sh Bash branch DENIES writes to paths it does not govern. Falsified 4 cases — a STATE.md write MENTIONING a guarded path plus an unrelated `mkdir` is denied (rc=2), while the same write without a verb passes; genuine guarded writes and read-only greps behave correctly. The branch fires on "command mentions the guarded tree" AND "command contains any write verb", never checking that the VERB OPERATES ON the guarded path — so an orchestrator's STATE.md record naming its own /km tasks gets blocked, silently rotting the recovery record. Fails CLOSED (blocks too much, never too little) so it is a nuisance with a clean workaround: use the Write tool, which is correctly path-scoped. NOT patched mid-train — a botched regex on a sacred-ground guard is a hole in the fence, and the evidence bar scales with irreversibility. The fix binds the verb to the path (strip heredoc BODIES before matching — a heredoc body is data, not an operand) and ships only with the falsification harness re-run green.

New § Rules law: an empty capture is a FAILED PROBE, never a quiet chat; never discard a capture's stderr. orchestrator.md already used the correct helper — the tool was right and only the watcher's doc was wrong.

- Fix: wave/orchestrator.md § O1.5 + wave/watcher.md § Round step 1 — the waiter now carries a TRACKING PROOF, not just a liveness check. A `&` inside `run_in_background` double-backgrounds the waiter: the wrapper exits 0 immediately, the real process reparents to PID 1, the harness tracks nothing, and its exit notification wakes nobody — a DEAF waiter that `kill -0` still reports as alive. Orchestrator launches direct and proves tracking (`ps -o ppid= -p $(cat wait.pid)`); the watcher's probe now checks PPID ≠ 1 rather than `kill -0` alone, which could not distinguish a healthy waiter from a deaf one.

- Fix: wave/watcher.md § Round step 1 — the waiter count uses the ANCHORED `pgrep -cf '^bash .*wave-wait\.sh'`. - Fix: wave/watcher.md § Round step 2 — LANE MOTION is now the FIRST probe of every round (STATE.md mtime age + queue remaining + worktree cut), ahead of every machinery check. The existing staleness rule fired only when "panes look busy", so it was structurally blind to the opposite and more common failure: an IDLE lane with a full queue, which sails through as "resting". An idle lane holding a non-empty queue is a DEAD lane until a holder is NAMED (boundary lock, Depends: edge, limit pause, or a ruling the orchestrator owes) — "idle-sanctioned"/"nothing owed" describe the orchestrator's state, never the lane's.

- Fix: scripts/wave-wait.sh — ROOT now resolves to the MAIN worktree via `git rev-parse --git-common-dir`, never from BASH_SOURCE. Every worktree is a full checkout carrying its own copy of the script, so a worktree-launched waiter resolved ROOT to THAT worktree and watched its empty spool: it delivered nothing and reported perfectly healthy while real builder pings piled up unconsumed in MAIN. The waiter can no longer be mis-addressed by a stray CWD or launch dir; with no git it dies loudly rather than watching nothing. This replaces the proposed "always use absolute paths" habit with a mechanism — the class's real fix.

- Fix: wave/watcher.md § Round step 2 — LANE MOTION derives from GROUND TRUTH, never from lanes.md. A wave is UN-LAUNCHED when its dir holds a manifest and no STATE.md; SHIPPED when `git log --grep="Wave: {name}"` finds its merge. lanes.md is a hand-written characterization that goes stale the moment a wave closes, and steering off its prose orders a RE-RUN of an already-merged wave. Also distinguishes an EXHAUSTED lane (all waves merged — wants a fill) from a STALLED one (un-launched waves left — wants a dispatch); the previous wording called both "dead".

- Fix: wave/orchestrator.md § O4 wake states — the bare `DEAD → respawn` now carries an evidence bar. A destructive act (respawn, /goal clear, Escape-recovery, kill) demands PROOF OF DEATH — zero turn-children AND no CPU AND no spinner, off a full-screen capture — never an inference from a marker: `cache-✗` is a CACHE state and says nothing about liveness, and a builder idle at a bare `❯` is HEALTHY-IDLE. The evidence bar scales with IRREVERSIBILITY: waiting costs latency and is recoverable; a respawn costs the session's whole context, crons and sub-agents, and is not. A partial view licenses only a fuller look, never an action.

- Fix: wave/watcher.md § Round step 2 — pick the instrument whose BROKEN state differs from its HEALTHY state FOR THE QUESTION ASKED. "Zero task reports" answers *did the builder finish anything*; it can never answer *is the builder working*, because a builder mid-first-task and a dead builder both have zero reports (same answer both ways = a coincidence detector that condemns a live build). Reports land on DONE, commits on CHECKPOINT — their absence during work is EXPECTED.
